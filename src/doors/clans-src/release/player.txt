Getting Started
===============================================================================

[1.1]  Overview
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The Clans is a text-based fantasy role-playing game for bulletin board
systems.  You lead a small clan of adventurers through a dangerous world
of mines, monsters, rival clans, village politics, and empire warfare.

Each day you have a limited number of actions.  You fight monsters in the
mines to earn gold and experience, train your members, buy equipment, manage
an empire, and compete against other players for control of the village.

If your BBS participates in an InterBBS league, you can even travel to
other villages on other BBSes and compete in a larger world.


[1.2]  Creating a Clan
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When you first enter the game, you will be asked whether you want to create
a clan.  If you accept, you will:

    1. Choose a clan name (up to 24 characters, must be unique).
    2. Create each member of your clan.  For every member you choose a
       race and a class.  Each combination produces different starting
       attributes, hit points, spell points, and known spells.
    3. After reviewing each member's stats, you can accept them or
       reroll for a different result.
    4. Name each member (up to 12 characters each).

Your first member is the clan leader and receives slightly higher starting
stats than the others.  All starting gold from your members is pooled into
your clan vault.


[1.3]  Your First Day
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
On your first day in the game, certain options are restricted.  You cannot
travel to other villages or trade at the market.  Use this day to explore
the mines, fight some monsters, and get familiar with the menus.

You start at mine level 0.  Fight monsters to earn experience and gold,
then visit the market to equip your members with weapons, armor, and
shields.  Check the training hall to spend any training points you have
earned.


[1.4]  Protection
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
New clans receive a period of protection during which other players
cannot attack your empire.  The number of protection days is set by
the sysop.  Once protection expires, any player can target your empire.


[1.5]  Game Settings
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
From the pre-game menu, press G to view the current game settings.  This
shows the daily limits and other configuration values the sysop has chosen
for this game, such as the number of mine fights and clan fights allowed
per day.


[1.6]  Daily Limits
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Each day you receive a fresh allotment of actions:

    - Mine fights:  the number of monster battles you can have.
    - Clan fights:  the number of player-vs-player battles you can have.
    - Quest:  you may go on one quest per day.
    - Chat:  you may chat with villagers up to 4 times per day.
    - Church services:  mass, prayer, and blessing are each once per day.
    - Economic changes:  the ruler may adjust each rate once per day.

These limits reset during the daily maintenance cycle.


Pre-Game Menu
===============================================================================

[2.1]  Pre-Game Commands
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Before entering the game proper, you see the pre-game menu.  The available
commands are:

    E   Enter the game.  Takes you to the main game menu.
    B   Read the bulletin.  Displays the sysop's bulletin board posting.
    V   Village stats.  Shows information about the current village.
    I   Instructions.  Displays the game help file.
    T   Today's news.  Read the news from today's game events.
    Y   Yesterday's news.  Read the news from the previous day.
    S   See scores.  Displays the clan rankings for this village.
    G   Game settings.  Shows the current game configuration.
    L   League scores.  Displays rankings across all villages in the
        InterBBS league (only available if InterBBS is enabled).
    H   Help.  Displays the general help screen.
    Q   Quit.  Exit The Clans and return to the BBS.


Main Game Menu
===============================================================================

[3.1]  In-Game Commands
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Once you enter the game, the main menu gives you access to all areas:

    E   Enter the Mines.  Go underground to fight monsters.
    M   Market.  Buy and sell weapons, armor, shields, and scrolls.
    C   Communications.  Read and write mail, post public messages.
    W   World Travel.  Travel to other villages (InterBBS only).
    T   Town Hall.  Visit the village government and voting booth.
    P   Empire.  Manage your clan's empire -- buildings, troops, and
        strategy.
    U   Church.  Attend mass, pray, receive blessings, and resurrect
        fallen members.
    H   Training Hall.  Train your members' attributes and manage
        your roster.
    A   Alliances.  Join or create alliances with other clans.
    V   View Stats.  Display your clan's current statistics.
    /   Chat.  Chat with other villagers in the street.
    N   Newbie Help.  Display beginner tips.
    !   Delete Clan.  Permanently delete your clan (confirmation
        required).
    Q   Quit.  Return to the pre-game menu.

The V (view stats), ? (redisplay menu), and Q (quit) commands are
available in nearly every menu throughout the game.


The Mines
===============================================================================

[4.1]  Overview
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The mines are where you spend most of your time.  You descend into the
mines to fight monsters for experience and gold, challenge other player
clans, and go on quests.

Each visit to the mines costs one fight from your daily allotment.  Choose
your mine level carefully -- deeper levels have tougher monsters but better
rewards.

The mine menu commands are:

    L   Fight monsters at your current mine level.
    F   Fight another player's clan.
    C   Change your mine level.
    G   Go on a quest (once per day).
    W   Who's here -- view the scores of other clans.
    V   View your clan's stats.
    /   Chat with other villagers in the mines.
    Q   Return to the main menu.


[4.2]  Mine Levels
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
You can set your mine level from 0 to 20.  Higher levels contain stronger
monsters that give more experience and gold.  If your clan is struggling,
drop to a lower level.  If fights are too easy, move deeper.

Your mine level is saved between sessions.  When you first create a clan,
you start at level 0.


[4.3]  Fighting Monsters
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Press L in the mines to fight a group of monsters.  The monsters you
encounter are randomly selected based on your mine level and your clan
members' levels.  Deeper mine levels and higher character levels produce
harder opponents.

Defeating monsters earns experience for your members (see section 5.7),
gold for your vault, and sometimes followers for your empire.  The number
of followers gained increases at higher mine levels.  A portion of your
new followers may be conscripted into your army based on the village's
conscription rate.


[4.4]  Fighting Other Clans
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Press F in the mines to challenge another player's clan.  You will be
shown a list of clans currently in the village.  Clan fights use your
clan fight allotment (separate from mine fights).

The combat system is the same as monster fights (see chapter 5), but
the stakes are higher -- winning earns more points and losing costs
more (see section 18.1).


[4.5]  Quests
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Press G in the mines to go on a quest.  You may go on one quest per day.
Quests are scripted adventures that may involve choices, NPC conversations,
special fights, and rewards.  The available quests depend on what quest
packs the sysop has installed.


Combat
===============================================================================

[5.1]  Overview
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Combat in The Clans is turn-based.  Your clan members and the enemy take
turns in an order determined by their agility and current health.  Each
round, every living combatant gets one action.  Combat continues until
one side is defeated, someone runs away, or 50 rounds have passed.


[5.2]  Combat Commands
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
During combat, when it is one of your members' turns, you choose an
action:

    A   Attack.  Strike the first available enemy.
    S   Specific attack.  Choose which enemy to target.
   1-9  Attack the enemy at that position number.
    K   Cast a spell.  Choose from your known spells and pick a target.
    E   Read a scroll.  Use a scroll item from your inventory.
    I   Parry.  Skip your turn and do nothing (sometimes called idle).
    R   Run away.  Attempt to flee the battle.
    P   Player stats.  Display the current member's full statistics.
    F   Fight to the death.  Commit to the battle -- no more running.
    D   Use default action.  Execute your preset action (see section
        5.6).
    ?   Combat help.  Display the combat help screen.

If you do not choose an action, the default is to attack (A).


[5.3]  Turn Order
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Each round, the game recalculates the order in which combatants act.
Characters with higher agility tend to act first.  Your current health
also matters -- wounded characters slow down.  There is a small random
factor so the order is not perfectly predictable.


[5.4]  Spells in Combat
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Press K to cast a spell.  You will be shown your known spells and asked
to choose one.  Each spell costs spell points (SP) to cast.  Some spells
target enemies (damage, incapacitation), some target allies (healing,
attribute modification), and some raise or banish undead creatures.

If you run out of SP, you cannot cast spells until your points are
restored (via the church blessing, leveling up, or waiting for the next
day's reset).


[5.5]  Scrolls
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Scrolls are single-use items that contain a spell.  Press E during combat
to read a scroll from your inventory.  The scroll is consumed after use.
Unlike casting a spell with K, reading a scroll does not cost any SP.


[5.6]  Default Actions
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Each member can have a default action set.  This is the action taken when
you press D during combat.  The default is a regular attack, but you can
set it to cast a specific spell automatically.  This speeds up combat
when you have a preferred tactic for each member.


[5.7]  Combat Outcomes
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
After combat ends, the results are tallied:

    - Experience is awarded based on the damage your members dealt and
      the enemies they defeated.  Knocking out or killing a stronger
      enemy awards more experience.
    - Gold may be looted from defeated monsters.
    - Items may be found on defeated enemies.
    - Clan points are awarded or deducted (see section 18.1).

If all your members are knocked unconscious or killed, you lose the
battle.  Dead members lose a small percentage of their maximum hit
points permanently and must be resurrected at the church (see section
11.3).  Unconscious members recover after the battle but must also
be revived at the church if you want them at full strength.


Character Stats and Leveling
===============================================================================

[6.1]  Attributes
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Each member has six attributes:

    Agility        Determines how quickly you act in combat and how
                   well you dodge enemy attacks.
    Dexterity      Affects your accuracy when striking an enemy.
    Strength       Governs how much damage your attacks deal.
    Wisdom         Influences your magical abilities and spell
                   effectiveness.
    Armor Strength Reduces the damage you take from enemy attacks.
    Charisma       Affects quest events and NPC dialogue.  Some
                   encounters may require a minimum Charisma score.
                   Also breaks tied elections: the clan whose members
                   have the highest Charisma (leader first) wins.  A
                   leader's Charisma also helps attract followers after
                   mine raids.

All attributes range from 0 to 100.  You improve them by spending
training points at the training hall (see section 10.2).


[6.2]  Leveling Up
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
As your members earn experience from combat, they will eventually level
up.  Each level requires more experience than the last.  The maximum
level is 100.

When a member levels up, they receive 10 to 13 training points which
you can spend at the training hall to improve their attributes or
increase their maximum hit points or spell points.


[6.3]  Status Effects
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Members can have the following status conditions:

    Here           Healthy and ready for action.
    Unconscious    Knocked out during combat.  Cannot fight until
                   revived at the church.
    Dead           Killed in combat.  Must be resurrected at the
                   church.  Suffers a permanent loss of some maximum
                   hit points.
    Ran Away       Fled from combat.  Returns to normal after the
                   battle.


The Market
===============================================================================

[7.1]  Overview
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The market is where you buy and sell equipment for your clan.  The
available commands are:

    W   Buy weapons.
    A   Buy armor.
    S   Buy shields.
    T   Trading.  Make trades with other clans (not available on
        your first day).
    P   Pawn shop.  Buy and sell used items.
    Z   Wizard's shop.  Buy scrolls and spell books.
    V   View stats.
    /   Chat with villagers in the market.
    Q   Return to the main menu.


[7.2]  Weapons, Armor, and Shields
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Press W, A, or S to browse the weapon, armor, or shield shops.  You
will see a list of available items with their stats and prices.  Prices
are affected by the village's goods and sales tax (GST) set by the
ruler.

To equip a purchased item, go to the item in your inventory and assign
it to a member.  Each member can equip one weapon, one set of armor,
and one shield.


[7.3]  Pawn Shop
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The pawn shop lets you buy used items at reduced prices or sell items
you no longer need.  The pawn shop is only available if the village has
one built (see section 12.4).  Higher pawn shop levels offer a better
selection.


[7.4]  Wizard's Shop
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The wizard's shop sells magical items.  It is only available if the
village has a wizard's shop built.  The commands are:

    S   Buy scrolls.  Single-use spell items for combat.
    B   Buy spell books.  Teach a permanent spell to one member.
    X   Examine item.  Identify an item in your inventory.  This
        costs 25% of the item's value.
    V   View stats.
    Q   Return to the market.


[7.5]  Trading
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Press T at the market to trade with another clan.  Trading is not
available on your first day.  You select a clan to trade with and
propose an exchange of gold, followers, or troops.


Items and Equipment
===============================================================================

[8.1]  Item Types
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Items in The Clans fall into several categories:

    Weapons        Increase your attack damage.
    Armor          Increase your defense.
    Shields        Provide additional protection.
    Scrolls        Single-use spells that can be read during combat.
    Spell Books    Permanently teach a spell to one member.
    Miscellaneous  Various other items with special properties.

Your clan can hold up to 30 items total.


[8.2]  Equipping Items
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
After purchasing or finding items, you must equip them on your members
for them to take effect.  Each member can have one weapon, one set of
armor, and one shield equipped at a time.  Unequipped items remain in
your clan's shared inventory.


[8.3]  Quality
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The quality of items available at the market varies day to day.  Market
quality fluctuates between average, good, very good, and excellent.
Better quality means better stats on the items for sale, but also
higher prices.


Magic
===============================================================================

[9.1]  Overview
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Magic plays an important role in The Clans.  Your members learn spells
from their race and class at creation, and can learn additional spells
by purchasing spell books from the wizard's shop.  Casting a spell
costs spell points (SP).


[9.2]  Spell Types
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Spells fall into six categories:

    Healing        Restores hit points to allies.
    Damage         Deals damage to enemies.
    Modification   Temporarily changes attributes or other effects.
    Incapacitation Prevents an enemy from acting on their next turn.
    Raise Undead   Summons an undead creature to fight on your side.
    Banish Undead  Destroys enemy undead creatures.

Some spells can only target allies, some only enemies.  The spell
description tells you what it does and who it can target.


[9.3]  Books and Scrolls
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Spell books permanently teach one member a new spell.  Once learned, the
member can cast that spell any time they have enough SP.

Scrolls are single-use items.  They can be read during combat (press E)
without spending any SP.  The scroll is consumed after one use.  Scrolls
are useful for casting spells your members have not learned or when you
are low on SP.


Training Hall
===============================================================================

[10.1]  Overview
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The training hall lets you improve your members' abilities and manage
your clan roster.  It is only available if the village has a training
hall built.  Higher training hall levels reduce training costs.

The commands are:

    T   Train a member.  Spend training points to improve attributes.
    A   Add a new member to your clan.
    L   Release a member from your clan.
    V   View stats.
    /   Chat with villagers in the training hall.
    Q   Return to the main menu.


[10.2]  Training Attributes
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When you train a member, you spend that member's training points to
increase their attributes.  The base cost in training points for each
attribute is:

    1 - Agility ............. 10 points
    2 - Dexterity ........... 10 points
    3 - Strength ............ 20 points
    4 - Wisdom .............. 10 points
    5 - Armor Strength ...... 30 points
    6 - Charisma ............ 40 points
    7 - Max Hit Points ...... 10 points
    8 - Max Spell Points .... 10 points

Each level of the village training hall reduces these costs by 1 point
(to a minimum of 1).  For example, at a level-3 training hall, training
agility costs only 7 points instead of 10.

Attributes are capped at 100.  Max HP and Max SP are capped at 15,000.
Training Max HP adds 3 to 11 points per level, and training Max SP
adds 2 to 6 points per level.


[10.3]  Adding and Releasing Members
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Press A to recruit a new permanent member.  You choose their race and
class just like during clan creation.  The maximum number of permanent
members depends on the game's configuration (up to 6).

Press L to release a member from your clan.  Released members are gone
permanently -- their equipment is lost.  Use this with caution.


The Church
===============================================================================

[11.1]  Overview
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The church provides healing and spiritual services.  It is only available
if the village has a church built.  Higher church levels unlock more
powerful services.

The commands are:

    M   Attend mass (once per day).
    P   Pray (once per day).
    B   Blessing -- fully restores your entire clan's SP (requires
        church level 2 or higher, once per day).
    U   Revive unconscious -- resurrect unconscious members (requires
        church level 2 or higher).
    D   Resurrect dead -- raise dead members (requires church level 3
        or higher).
    V   View stats.
    /   Chat with villagers in the church.
    Q   Return to the main menu.


[11.2]  Services
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Attending mass and praying are daily rituals that may grant small
blessings or bonuses.  Each can be done once per day.

The blessing (B) fully restores all present members' spell points to
their maximums.  This is very useful when your clan has exhausted its
SP during combat.

Reviving unconscious members (U) and resurrecting dead members (D)
cost gold from your clan's vault.  The cost depends on the member's
level.


[11.3]  Resurrection Costs
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The cost to restore a fallen member depends on their level:

    Revive Unconscious:  (Level + 1) x 300 gold per member.
    Resurrect Dead:      (Level + 1) x 750 gold per member.

For example, reviving a level-4 unconscious member costs 1,500 gold.
Resurrecting a level-4 dead member costs 3,750 gold.

The number of resurrections available per day depends on the church
level.  Build a higher-level church to restore more members each day.


Town Hall
===============================================================================

[12.1]  Overview
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The town hall is the center of village government.  What you can do here
depends on whether your clan rules the village or not.  All players can
vote, deposit gold, and view the village empire.  The ruler has additional
powers to build structures, set taxes, and manage the village.

When the village has no ruler, any clan can claim rule immediately by
accepting the prompt to govern (see section 12.8 for details).  This
bypasses the voting process entirely.


[12.2]  Citizen Menu
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If you are not the ruler, the town hall commands are:

    D   Deposit gold into the village vault.
    E   Donate resources to the village empire.
    W   Write a message to the ruler.
    B   Voting booth -- cast your vote for the next ruler.
    S   Empire stats -- view the village empire's statistics.
    P   Public discussion board.
    H   Help for citizens.
    V   View your clan stats.
    /   Chat with villagers in the town hall.
    Q   Return to the main menu.


[12.3]  Ruler Menu
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If your clan rules the village, you have these commands:

    S   Structures -- build and upgrade village buildings.
    E   Economics -- set tax rates and manage the vault.
    P   Manage empire -- edit the village empire (troops, buildings).
    R   Conscription rate -- set the military draft rate (once per
        day, 0-20%).
    C   Colors -- change the village's color scheme.
    L   Flag -- customize the village flag colors.
    M   Make announcement -- broadcast a message to all players.
    B   Voting booth -- view current election standings.
    D   Public discussion board.
    T   Toggle empire stats -- show or hide empire info for citizens.
    H   Help for rulers.
    V   View your clan stats.
    !   Abdicate -- give up rule of the village.
    /   Chat with villagers in the town hall.
    Q   Return to the main menu.


[12.4]  Structures
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The ruler can build and upgrade village structures.  Each structure
unlocks or improves a service available to all players.  Building costs
gold from the village vault.

    C   Church         Healing, blessings, resurrections.
    T   Training Hall  Member training and recruitment.
    P   Pawn Shop      Used item trading.
    Z   Wizard's Shop  Scrolls, books, and item identification.
    F   Smithy         Improves weapon and armor quality at market.

Each structure has multiple levels.  Higher levels unlock better services
and items.  Check the vault (S in the build menu) to see how much gold
is available before building.


[12.5]  Economics
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The ruler controls the village economy through the economics menu:

    T   Tax rate (0-50%, once per day).  Income tax on player
        earnings.
    G   GST (0-50%, once per day).  Goods and sales tax on market
        purchases.
    D   Deposit clan gold into the village vault.
    W   Withdraw gold from the village vault (up to 3 times per day).

Higher tax rates generate more revenue for the village but make the
village less attractive to players.  A wise ruler balances income with
player satisfaction.

The village vault funds all building construction and other village
expenses.  Both the ruler and citizens can deposit gold, but only the
ruler can withdraw.


[12.6]  Voting
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
All players can vote for who should rule the village.  Press B in the
town hall to enter the voting booth.

    C   Change your vote.  Select a different clan as your choice
        for ruler.
    V   View stats.
    Q   Leave the voting booth.

The voting booth displays the top 10 candidates and their vote counts.
The clan with the most votes when the current ruler abdicates becomes the
new ruler.  If two or more clans tie, the winner is determined by comparing
each clan's member Charisma starting from the leader -- the clan whose
members have the highest Charisma wins.  If all tied clans have identical
Charisma at every slot, the winner is chosen at random.

Important: If the ruler is ousted by military attack rather than
abdication, the voting process is bypassed entirely.  Any clan can claim
rule immediately by visiting the town hall (see section 12.8).


[12.7]  Abdication
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
A ruler can voluntarily give up control of the village by pressing !
in the town hall.  This resets the village to having no ruler.  The
clan that abdicated loses 100 points.  Abdication should not be taken
lightly.

After you abdicate, you cannot claim rule again until the next day.  This
prevents rapid cycling of power.  Note: This restriction only applies to
voluntary abdication.  If your clan is ousted by a military attack, you
may claim rule again immediately (see section 12.8).


[12.8]  Claiming Rule Without an Election
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When the village has no ruler, any clan can claim rule immediately by
visiting the town hall.  The game will prompt you: "Does your clan wish to
rule the village?"  If you answer yes, you become the ruler instantly
without waiting for an election.  This grants you 20 points and all ruler
powers.

A village has no ruler in these situations:

    1. A previous ruler abdicated voluntarily.
    2. A previous ruler was ousted by a successful military attack.
    3. The village is brand new.

This claim mechanic bypasses the normal voting process (see section 12.6).
Unlike voting, there is no time delay or Charisma tiebreaker -- the first
clan to accept the town hall's offer becomes ruler.

Restriction: If your clan was the previous ruler and abdicated, you cannot
claim rule again until the next day.  This prevents rapid cycling of power.
However, if your clan was ousted by military attack instead, you may claim
rule immediately if you reach the town hall first.


Empires
===============================================================================

[13.1]  Overview
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Every clan has an empire consisting of land, buildings, and an army.
The village itself also has an empire managed by the ruler.  Through
your empire, you develop land, construct buildings, train troops, and
wage war against rival villages or clans.


[13.2]  Buildings
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
There are 10 types of empire buildings:

    Barracks            House your troops.  Each barracks supports
                        20 footmen, 10 axemen, or 5 knights.
                        Cost: 2,500 gold.

    Walls               Strengthen your defenses against attacks.
                        Cost: 1,000 gold.

    Towers              Provide ranged defense bonuses.
                        Cost: 1,500 gold.

    Steel Mill          Required to train axemen and knights.
                        Cost: 3,000 gold.

    Stables             Required (along with a steel mill) to train
                        knights.  Cost: 5,000 gold.

    Intelligence Agency Allows you to spy on other empires.  More
                        agencies increase your success chance.
                        Cost: 1,000 gold.

    Security Center     Defends against enemy spies.  More centers
                        increase your chance of catching spies.
                        Cost: 1,000 gold.

    Gymnasium           Boosts your army's training and rating.
                        Cost: 4,500 gold.

    Developer's Hall    Reduces the cost of developing new land.
                        Cost: 3,000 gold.

    Shops               Generate income for your empire.
                        Cost: 5,000 gold.

Each building uses land and energy.  You must develop land before you
can build on it.


[13.3]  Troops
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
There are three types of troops, each with different strengths:

    Footmen     15 gold each.  Basic soldiers.  Require only
                barracks.  Well-rounded but not specialized.

    Axemen      30 gold each.  Stronger attackers.  Require barracks
                and a steel mill.  Hit harder than footmen.

    Knights     60 gold each.  Elite mounted warriors.  Require
                barracks, a steel mill, and stables.  The fastest
                and strongest offensive units.

Your barracks capacity limits how many troops you can maintain.  Each
barracks supports 20 footmen, 10 axemen, or 5 knights (or any
combination within capacity).

Followers are civilians who join your empire after mine victories.
They can be conscripted into your army based on the village
conscription rate.


[13.4]  Strategy
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Your empire has a battle strategy that affects how your army fights:

    Attack Length      How many rounds your army presses the attack
                       (1-10).
    Attack Intensity   How aggressively your troops fight: light,
                       moderate, or heavy.
    Loot Level         How much you try to steal: 10%, 20%, or 30%.
    Defend Length      How long your army holds a defensive position
                       (1-10).
    Defend Intensity   How strongly your troops defend: light,
                       moderate, or heavy.

Higher intensity deals more damage but also makes your troops more
vulnerable.  Find a balance that suits your army composition.


[13.5]  Attacking
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
You can launch attacks against other villages, alliances, or clans.
When attacking, you choose:

    1. Your target.
    2. Which troops to send (footmen, axemen, knights).
    3. An attack goal:

    Oust Ruler     Attempt to take control of the target village
                   by removing its current ruler.
    Steal Land     Seize a portion of the target's developed land.
    Steal Gold     Raid the target's vault for gold.
    Destroy        Damage the target's buildings and infrastructure.

The outcome depends on the strength and composition of both armies,
the buildings involved (walls and towers help defenders), and the
strategies of both sides.


[13.6]  Spying
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If you have built intelligence agencies, you can send spies to
gather information about other empires.  Each spy attempt costs
1,000 gold.

If the spy succeeds, you see the target's full empire statistics --
buildings, troops, gold, and strategy.  If the spy fails, you learn
nothing, and the target may be notified that they caught a spy from
your clan.

The more intelligence agencies you build, the higher your chance of
success.  The target's security centers work against you -- more
security centers means a higher chance your spy is caught.


Alliances
===============================================================================

[14.1]  Overview
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Alliances are groups of clans that band together for mutual benefit.
Alliance members can coordinate their efforts in village politics and
empire warfare.


[14.2]  Creating and Joining
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Press A from the main menu to enter the alliance screen.  You will
see a list of existing alliances.  Select one to view its members
or request to join.

Each clan can create one alliance and belong to up to 5 alliances
total.  The game supports a maximum of 16 alliances.

To create a new alliance, select the option to form one and give it
a name.  Other players can then join your alliance through the
alliance menu.


Communications
===============================================================================

[15.1]  Overview
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The communications menu lets you send and receive messages.  Press C
from the main menu to access it:

    C   Check mail.  Read private messages sent to your clan.
    W   Write mail.  Send a private message to another clan.
    P   Public mail.  Post to the public message board.
    U   Update last read.  Set your last-read message pointer.
    G   Global message.  Post a message visible across all villages
        in the InterBBS league (only available if InterBBS is
        enabled).
    V   View stats.
    Q   Return to the main menu.


[15.2]  Mail
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Private mail is delivered between clans on the same village.  Use
"Write mail" to compose a message and choose a recipient from the
clan list.  Use "Check mail" to read messages others have sent you.

The "Update last read" option lets you mark all current messages as
read so that only new messages appear next time.


[15.3]  Public Messages and Chat
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Public mail is posted to a message board visible to all players in
the village.  Use this for announcements, challenges, or general
discussion.

Chat (/) is available in most locations -- the street, mines, market,
church, training hall, and town hall.  Each location has its own chat
room.  You can chat up to 4 times per day across all locations.


NPCs
===============================================================================

[16.1]  Overview
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Non-player characters (NPCs) wander through the village and can be
found in various locations.  They may have useful information, offer
quests, or simply provide flavor to the world.


[16.2]  Talking to NPCs
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
When you chat with villagers (press /) in any location, you may
encounter NPCs.  You can talk to them about specific topics to learn
things or trigger quest events.

NPCs wander between six zones:  the church, the street, the market,
the town hall, the training hall, and the mines.  Not every NPC is
in every zone at all times -- they move around during maintenance.
Check different locations if you are looking for a particular NPC.


World Travel
===============================================================================

[17.1]  Overview
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If the sysop has enabled InterBBS mode, your village is part of a
larger league of villages hosted on different BBSes.  World travel
lets you move your clan between these villages.

Press W from the main menu to access the world travel menu:

    O   Other villages.  View the list of villages in the league.
    T   Travel.  Choose a village to travel to (not available on
        your first day).
    S   Current trip info.  See your travel status.
    H   Help.  Information about village travel.
    V   View stats.
    Q   Return to the main menu.


[17.2]  Traveling
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
To travel, press T and select a destination village.  Your clan data
is packaged up and sent to the destination BBS during the next
maintenance cycle.  You will be able to play at the new village the
following day.

While traveling, you cannot play at your current village.  Plan your
travels carefully.

Travel is not available on your first day in the game.  You must play
at least one day locally before you can travel.


Scoring
===============================================================================

[18.1]  Point Values
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Your clan earns or loses points based on your actions.  Points
determine your ranking on the scoreboard.

    Win a clan fight .................. +20 points
    Lose a clan fight ................. -15 points
    Run from a clan fight ............. -20 points
    Win a monster fight ............... +10 points
    Lose or run from a monster fight .. -10 points
    Become village ruler .............. +20 points
    Abdicate the throne .............. -100 points

Running from a clan fight is penalized more harshly than losing.
Standing your ground, even in defeat, costs you fewer points than
fleeing.

These point changes appear in the daily news, so all players can
track the rise and fall of the clans.


Miscellaneous
===============================================================================

[19.1]  Deleting Your Clan
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Press ! from the main menu to permanently delete your clan.  You will
be asked to confirm this choice.  Once deleted, your clan, all its
members, items, gold, empire, and alliance memberships are gone
forever.  This cannot be undone.


[19.2]  Disbanding
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
If all your members are killed and you cannot afford to resurrect
them, your clan may be effectively out of the game.  In this case,
you may need to delete your clan and start over.

A disbanded clan's name is recorded to prevent immediate re-creation
with the same name.  If your BBS participates in an InterBBS league,
deletion notifications are sent to all other villages.


[19.3]  News
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The game keeps a daily news log of significant events:  clan fights,
new clans, ruler changes, empire attacks, and more.  Access the news
from the pre-game menu:

    T   Today's news.
    Y   Yesterday's news.

Check the news regularly to keep up with village politics and see
who has been fighting whom.


[19.4]  Viewing Stats
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Press V from almost any menu to view your clan's current statistics.
This shows:

    - Your clan name, points, and ranking.
    - Each member's name, level, HP, SP, status, and equipment.
    - Your clan's gold.
    - Your empire summary.
    - Your alliance memberships.

Use this often to keep track of your members' health, equipment, and
training point balances.
